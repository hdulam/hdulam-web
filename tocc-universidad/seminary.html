About the Seminary

Forming Clergy for the
Apostolic Church

The Seminary of St. Matthew of South Mimms serves as the principal center for clergy formation within The Old Catholic University. As an academic institute of the University, the Seminary is dedicated to the preparation of candidates for Holy Orders and to the continuing theological and pastoral formation of clergy.

Rooted in the apostolic tradition of the Church, the Seminary integrates spiritual, intellectual, pastoral, and liturgical formation in order to prepare ministers who are faithful in doctrine, reverent in sacramental life, and wise in pastoral service.

Through its close relationship with the University, the Seminary supports the formation of deacons, priests, and church leaders for service within The Old Catholic Church and for broader ecclesial ministry in communities seeking serious theological preparation.

Pillars of Formation

Spiritual Formation

Prayer, sacramental participation, and the cultivation of a life centered on Christ through the rhythm of the Church's liturgical year.

Intellectual Formation

Rigorous theological study rooted in Scripture, Tradition, and the Fathers — developing minds capable of sound teaching.

Pastoral Formation

Wisdom, humility, and compassion cultivated through mentorship, guided ministry experience, and reflection.

Liturgical Formation

Reverence and fidelity in the celebration of the sacraments and liturgy of the Church.

Formation Path

Preparation for
Holy Orders

Ordained Ministry Preparation

The Seminary provides formation for candidates discerning a call to the Diaconate, Priesthood, or Episcopal orders within The Old Catholic Church. Formation is integrated with the theological programs of the University, ensuring candidates are prepared in all dimensions of ministry.

Continuing Clergy Formation

The Seminary also serves the ongoing formation of ordained clergy already serving in ministry. Through advanced theological study and pastoral reflection, clergy are accompanied in deepening their theological understanding and pastoral wisdom throughout their ministry.

Theological Integration

Seminary formation is fully integrated with the academic programs of the University. Candidates for ordination pursue theological degrees alongside their pastoral and spiritual formation, ensuring that theological learning and ministerial preparation develop together.

Apostolic Succession

All ordinations within The Old Catholic Church take place within a clear line of apostolic succession, traceable to the historic episcopate of the undivided Church. The Seminary preserves this heritage as an essential element of its formation of candidates for Holy Orders.

International Reach

A Global Community
of Formation

The Old Catholic University and its Seminary serve an international community of students. Seminary formation is available in three languages, supporting the Church's global mission of theological education and clerical formation.
